Estranged Students & Care Leavers

If you're estranged or care experienced, contact your university's accommodation or student welfare team early — ideally before you accept your offer. Many universities offer year-round accommodation for students who can't return home in the holidays, as well as housing bursaries or priority allocation for care leavers. These aren't always advertised, so ask directly.

Stand Alone has a directory of university support contacts who can help you navigate accommodation and welfare entitlements.

Year-Round University Accommodation

Many universities offer extended accommodation to estranged students and care leavers who have no home to return to in the holidays. These are not always advertised — contact your accommodation office or student welfare team directly and ask about:

  • Year-round or holiday accommodation
  • Priority allocation for care leavers and estranged students
  • Housing bursaries or emergency housing funds
  • Care leaver support packages
  • Emergency mid-year housing if your circumstances change

Stand Alone's HE contacts directory can help you find the right person at your university to speak to.

Many landlords require a guarantor — someone who agrees to pay your rent if you can’t. For estranged students and care leavers without family support, this can be one of the biggest barriers to renting privately. You have more options than you might think.

What a Guarantor Does

A guarantor agrees to cover rent or damage costs if you can’t. Shelter’s guide explains your rights and what landlords can and cannot require.

Shelter – Guarantors for private renters
Citizens Advice – If you need a guarantor to start renting

Alternatives to a Family Guarantor

  • Your university may be able to act as a guarantor — ask accommodation or student services.
  • A housemate’s parent may agree if appropriate.
  • If comfortable, explain your situation to your landlord directly — some are willing to find solutions.
  • Housing Hand Ltd offers UK-wide guarantor services, with some reduced schemes through partner universities.

Knowing your rights as a tenant makes a real difference. Your landlord has legal obligations, and understanding them helps you protect yourself if things go wrong.

Deposit Protection

Your landlord must protect your deposit in a government-approved scheme and give you the details within 30 days. If they don't, you may be entitled to compensation.

Approved schemes include:

  • Deposit Protection Service
  • MyDeposits
  • Tenancy Deposit Scheme

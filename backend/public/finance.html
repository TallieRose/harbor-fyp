You deserve financial stability.

Financial Guidance & Funding

Navigating the benefits system and affording medication or childcare can be exhausting. You don't have to figure this out alone — the resources below are trusted starting points.

Citizens Advice

We recommend Citizens Advice first for all benefits support. They provide free, impartial advice on benefits, tax credits, council tax, housing costs, national insurance and more — in person, online or by phone.

Citizens Advice – full support

Help with Healthcare Costs

If you're on a low income, you may qualify for help with NHS prescriptions, dental treatment, sight tests and travel to appointments.

NHS help with health costs

Turn2us

Turn2us helps people find welfare benefits and grants they may be entitled to. Their benefits calculator is free and straightforward. It's useful if you're unsure what support you qualify for, especially if you have caring responsibilities or are working part-time alongside studying.

Turn2us – Get Support
Turn2us – full-time students and benefits

Parents’ Learning Allowance

If you’re a full-time student with children, Parents’ Learning Allowance can provide extra financial support on top of your student loan.

  • Does not need to be repaid
  • Paid on top of student finance
  • Will not affect benefits
Parents’ Learning Allowance – GOV.UK

Childcare Grant

You may also be able to claim a Childcare Grant if you have children under 15 (or under 17 if they have special educational needs). It can cover up to 85% of your weekly childcare costs and does not need to be repaid. This is separate from the 30 hours free childcare and can be claimed alongside it.

Childcare Grant – GOV.UK
What you’ll get – GOV.UK

DSAs cover additional study costs due to disability, long-term illness or mental health conditions.

  • No repayment required
  • Based on individual needs
  • Paid alongside other finance
https://www.gov.uk/disabled-students-allowances-dsas

Does not need to be repaid.

Student Finance often isn’t enough to cover all your costs. Grants and scholarships don’t need to be repaid, so it’s always worth searching for what you might be entitled to before taking on extra debt.

University Hardship Funds & Bursaries

Most universities have their own hardship funds, bursaries and emergency grants for students in financial difficulty. Many have specific support for estranged students and care leavers, but these aren’t always advertised widely. Ask your student services, welfare team or students’ union directly. It’s worth asking even if you’re unsure whether you qualify.

Student finance extra help – GOV.UK

If you've grown up in care or have care experience, there is support designed specifically for you. Many organisations and universities now have dedicated packages — it's worth exploring what you may be entitled to before, during and after university.

Propel into Education

Propel is a free comparison tool that lets you search and compare the support packages offered by different universities for care-experienced and estranged students, including bursaries, extended accommodation and dedicated welfare contacts. Useful whether you're still choosing a university or already enrolled and wondering if you're receiving everything you're entitled to.

Become

Become is a charity for children in care and young care leavers in England. Their website brings together practical information about funding, rights and support — including an overview of bursaries available to care leavers and a link to the Propel comparison tool.

Stand Alone – Support at Your University

Stand Alone's university support page explains what good support for estranged students looks like and helps you understand what you can expect and ask for at your institution. Their HE contacts directory can also connect you with a named person at your university.

Stand Alone – support at your university

If you’re estranged from your parents or family, you may be able to apply for student finance as an independent student. This means your funding is assessed on your own circumstances, not your parents’ income, which typically results in a higher maintenance loan.

Applying as an Estranged Student

You’ll need to provide evidence of your estrangement to Student Finance England. Stand Alone has a clear, practical guide written specifically for students in this situation, including a checklist of what to send and how to word your application. This process can feel daunting, but the guide makes it manageable.

Support & Organisations

If you're in crisis or need to talk to someone today, these services are free and available around the clock. You don't need to be in immediate danger to reach out. Any difficult moment is enough reason.

Samaritans

Free to call any time, day or night, for anyone who needs to talk. You don't have to be suicidal to call. If something is weighing on you, that's enough.

Call 116 123 (free, 24/7) or email jo@samaritans.org

Shout

Free, confidential, 24/7 crisis text support. You can text from anywhere and the conversation is completely private.

Text SHOUT to 85258

Student Space — Text Support

Student Space offers a text support line specifically for students, available evenings and weekends when other services may be harder to reach.

Student Space – urgent text message support

Papyrus HOPELINE247

Confidential support for young people struggling with thoughts of suicide, and advice for anyone concerned about a young person. Available 24 hours a day.

Call 0800 068 4141, text 07860 039967, or email pat@papyrus-uk.org

Papyrus HOPELINE247

Experiencing mental health difficulties at university can feel overwhelming, especially if you feel like you're managing it alone. Student Minds and Student Space both exist to help you find the right support at your institution.

Student Minds

Student Minds is the UK's student mental health charity. Their website brings together information about support programmes and services available at your university, and resources for understanding your own mental health.

Student Minds – support for me

Student Space

Student Space is a dedicated mental health support hub for students in the UK. It can help you find your university's counselling and welfare services, and offers its own resources and tools alongside them.

Student Space
Student Space – support at your university

Loneliness at university is common. For estranged students or those without family support, it can feel particularly acute. You're not unusual for feeling this way. These resources are for anyone who feels disconnected, isolated, or like they don't quite fit in.

Student Space – Loneliness at University

Student Space has a thoughtful guide on loneliness specifically for students, with practical suggestions and reminders that it's far more common than it looks.

Student Space – overcoming loneliness at university

Student Space – Moving Away from Home

Moving away from home brings its own emotional weight, especially if home was difficult, unsafe, or absent. Student Space's guide covers the adjustment period and how to settle in when things feel hard.

Student Space – moving away from home and mental health

UCAS – Estrangement and Emotional Wellbeing

UCAS has written specifically about the emotional experience of estrangement at university, including managing difficult feelings around family events, holidays and milestones when you're doing it alone.

UCAS – applying to university if you are experiencing estrangement

Mind – Connecting with Other Students

Mind's student hub has practical advice on making connections and building community at university, even if socialising feels difficult right now.

Mind – connecting with other students

Mind – Managing Stress and Building Resilience

If stress is affecting your sense of connection or your ability to reach out, Mind's resilience and stress guides may help you find a steadier foundation.

Mind – managing stress and building resilience

Nightline

Nightline listening services are run by trained student volunteers, for students. They are anonymous, non-judgmental, and available overnight, when many other services are closed and things can feel hardest. Check if your university has a Nightline.

Nightline – listening services

Off The Record (OTR)

Free counselling for 14–25 year olds, including support for young carers, refugees and online counselling. Particularly supportive if you're navigating difficult family circumstances.

UMHAN for Students

University Mental Health Advisers Network. Encourages awareness of your rights, resources and support networks at university.

UMHAN – for students

Students Against Depression

Peer-reviewed resources and advice for students experiencing low mood, depression or suicidal thinking.

Students Against Depression

Togetherall

Togetherall is a safe, anonymous online support community moderated 24/7 by mental health professionals. It's a space to share how you're feeling without anyone knowing who you are. Many universities offer free access, so check if yours does.
